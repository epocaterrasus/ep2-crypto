Latency Simulation\n\n### Does Latency Matter at 5-Minute Frequency?\n\n**Short answer: less than you think, but more than zero.**\n\nAt 5-minute bars, a 100ms latency vs 10ms latency rarely matters — the next bar open is 5 minutes away. But there are scenarios where it does matter:\n\n1. **Signal races**: If your signal fires right at bar close and you need to get an order in before the next bar's first trade, latency matters.\n2. **Stop-loss execution**: When a stop triggers mid-bar, 500ms can mean the difference between filling at $50,000 and $49,800 in a crash.\n3. **Cross-exchange arbitrage signals**: If your NQ signal informs BTC trades, the delay between NQ data arriving and your BTC order reaching Binance matters.\n\n```python\n@dataclass\nclass LatencyModel:\n    \"\"\"\n    Models the full pipeline latency from signal to fill.\n    \n    Components:\n    1.
