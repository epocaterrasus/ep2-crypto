Alpha Research Workflow\n\n### How Quant Researchers Iterate\n\nThe standard workflow at quant funds follows a structured pipeline:\n\n#### Stage 1: Ideation\n- **Sources**: Academic papers (SSRN, arXiv quantitative finance), market microstructure observations, anomaly detection on historical data, cross-asset analogies.\n- **Tools**: Jupyter notebooks, pandas, matplotlib/plotly.\n- **Output**: A hypothesis (\"orderbook imbalance predicts 5-minute returns\").\n\n#### Stage 2: Exploratory Data Analysis\n- **Process**: Load historical data, compute the proposed feature, visually inspect correlations, check for lookahead bias.\n- **Tools**: Jupyter, pandas, seaborn, DuckDB for large datasets.\n- **Key checks**:\n  - Is the signal statistically significant?\n  - Is it stable across time periods?\n  - Is there survivorship bias?\n  - Is it already well-known (alpha decay)?\n- **Time**: Hours to days.\n\n#### Stage 3: Backtest\n- **Process**: Implement the full strategy including entry/exit logic, position sizing, transaction costs.\n- **Critical**: Use realistic assumptions:\n  - Include exchange fees (maker/taker)\n  - Model slippage (especially for large orders or illiquid periods)\n  - Account for latency (delay between signal and execution)\n  - Use walk-forward validation (train on past, test on future, roll forward)\n- **Tools**: Custom backtesting engine, vectorbt, or QuantConnect/Lean.\n- **Anti-patterns to avoid**:\n  - Optimizing on the full dataset (overfitting)\n  - Ignoring transaction costs\n  - Not testing across different market regimes (bull, bear, sideways, high vol, low vol)\n- **Time**: Days to weeks.\n\n#### Stage 4: Paper Trading\n- **Process**: Run the strategy live but with simulated orders. Check daily PnL → reject if daily loss limit hit\n  2. Check current exposure → reject if max position reached\n  3. Check recent trade count → reject if over limit\n  4. Calculate position size based on Kelly criterion or fixed fraction\n  5. Set stop-loss and take-profit at order placement time\n  6. Log everything\n\nEvery minute:\n  1. Check unrealized PnL of open positions\n  2. Check max drawdown\n  3. If any limit breached → close all positions, halt system, alert\n```\n\n#### Kelly Criterion for Position Sizing\n\nThe Kelly Criterion gives the theoretically optimal bet size:\n\n```\nKelly fraction = (win_rate * avg_win - (1 - win_rate) * avg_loss) / avg_win\n```\n\nIn practice, use **half-Kelly** or **quarter-Kelly** because:\n- Your win rate and avg win/loss estimates have uncertainty\n- Kelly assumes you can handle the volatility (you can't)\n- Half-Kelly gives ~75% of the returns with ~50% of the drawdown\n\nExample: If win rate = 55%, avg win = 1.2%, avg loss = 1.0%:\n- Full Kelly = (0.55 * 1.2 - 0.45 * 1.0) / 1.2 = 17.5% of capital per trade\n- Half Kelly = 8.75%\n- Quarter Kelly = 4.4% (recommended starting point)\n\n#### Practical Risk Parameters for Your System\n\nStarting with $10,000 capital:\n\n| Parameter | Value |\n|-----------|-------|\n| Position size per trade | $400-500 (4-5%) |\n| Daily loss limit | $200-300 (2-3%) |\n| Weekly loss limit | $500 (5%) |\n| Max drawdown halt | $1,500 (15%) |\n| Stop loss per trade | $10-15 (2-3% of position) |\n| Max open positions | 1 |\n| Max trades per day | 20 |\n\n---\n\n## 10. Execution Algorithms\n\n### When to Use Market vs Limit Orders\n\nFor 5-minute signals, execution matters more than you might think. Fees and slippage compound over thousands of trades.\n\n#### Market Orders\n- **Pros**: Guaranteed fill. Immediate execution. Simple.\n- **Cons**: Pay taker fees (higher). Subject to slippage. In thin orderbooks, can move the market.\n- **Use when**: Signal is strong and time-sensitive. Small position relative to orderbook depth. Exiting a losing position (urgency).\n\n#### Limit Orders\n- **Pros**: Pay maker fees (lower or zero). Control execution price. No slippage.\n- **Cons**: May not fill. Adverse selection (fills when price moves against you). Adds complexity.\n- **Use when**: Signal confidence is moderate. Position size is large relative to orderbook depth. You have time within your 5-minute window.\n\n#### Fee Comparison (Binance Example)\n\n| Order Type | Maker Fee | Taker Fee | Annual Impact (20 trades/day, $500/trade) |\n|-----------|----------|----------|------------------------------------------|\n| Market (taker) | - | 0.10% | $3,650 |\n| Limit (maker) | 0.02% | - | $730 |\n| BNB discount (taker) | - | 0.075% | $2,738 |\n\n**The difference between maker and taker fees is ~$2,900/year for a modest trading frequency.** This is a significant drag on returns.\n\n### Execution Algorithms\n\n#### TWAP (Time-Weighted Average Price)\n- **How it works**: Split a large order into equal-sized chunks, execute at regular intervals over a time period.\n- **Example**: To buy $5,000 of BTC over 5 minutes, place 5 orders of $1,000 every minute.\n- **Best for**: Reducing market impact on large orders.\n- **Relevance to you**: Probably overkill for $500 positions, but useful if you scale up.\n\n#### VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price)\n- **How it works**: Split order proportional to expected volume at each time interval. Execute more during high-volume periods.\n- **Best for**: Matching the market's average price.\n- **Relevance to you**: More relevant for larger positions ($10K+).\n\n#### Iceberg Orders\n- **How it works**: Display only a small portion of a large limit order. As the visible portion fills, reveal more.\n- **Best for**: Hiding your full intention from other market participants.\n- **Relevance to you**: Only if trading size is large enough to move the market (unlikely at $500/trade for BTC).\n\n### Practical Execution Strategy for Your System\n\n**Recommended approach — Aggressive Limit Order**:\n\n1. When signal fires, check the current orderbook.\n2. Place a limit order at 1-2 ticks above the best bid (for buys) or below the best ask (for sells). This gives you a high probability of fill at close to maker fees.\n3. If not filled within 10-30 seconds, convert to a market order (you need to be in the trade before the 5-min window progresses too far).\n4. Track fill rate — if your limit orders fill >80% of the time, you're saving significant fees.\n\n```\nSignal fires at T+0\n  T+0s:   Place limit buy at best_ask - 0.01%\n  T+10s:  If not filled, move limit to best_ask\n  T+20s:  If still not filled, convert to market order\n  T+30s:  Position should be open\n```\n\nThis approach captures maker fees most of the time while ensuring you don't miss trades.\n\n---\n\n## 13.
