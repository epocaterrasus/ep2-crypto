The Variance Drain Problem\n\n### The Core Formula\n\nThis is one of the most underappreciated concepts in trading math.\n\n**Geometric return ≈ Arithmetic return - (Variance / 2)**\n\nExample:\n- Day 1: +10%, Day 2: -10%\n- Arithmetic average: 0%\n- Actual result: $100 → $110 → $99. **Lost 1%.**\n- Variance = 0.01, Variance/2 = 0.005. Geometric drag ≈ -0.5% per period.\n\n### Application to 5-Min BTC Trading\n\nWith per-trade returns following approximately:\n- Mean: +0.0375% (from the 55% WR, 1.5:1 R:R calculation above)\n- SD: 1.244% per trade\n\n**Variance drag per trade:** `(0.01244²) / 2 = 0.0000774 = 0.00774%`\n\nOver 30 trades/day: `0.00774% × 30 = 0.232%/day` of variance drag.\n\nYour arithmetic expected daily return is `0.375% × 30 = 11.25%` but geometric expected return is approximately `11.25% - 0.232% = 11.02%`. At this level, variance drag is small relative to returns. **But this only holds if trades are independent.**\n\n### When Variance Drain Becomes Deadly\n\nIf trades are **correlated** (which they are in trending BTC markets), effective variance is much higher:\n\nWith 0.5 correlation between adjacent trades:\n```\nPortfolio_Variance = n × σ² + n(n-1) × ρ × σ²\n= 30 × 0.01244² + 30 × 29 × 0.5 × 0.01244²\n= 30 × 0.0001548 + 435 × 0.0001548 × 0.5\n= 0.004644 + 0.03367\n= 0.03831\nVariance_drag = 0.03831 / 2 = 1.916% per day\n```\n\nNow the drag is **1.916%/day**, which destroys most of the 11.25% arithmetic return. Effective geometric daily return drops to ~9.33%.\n\n### Position Sizing to Minimize Variance Drain\n\nThe **optimal position size** that maximizes geometric growth (Kelly) inherently accounts for variance drain. Undersizing costs little; oversizing destroys returns.\n\n### Practical Formula for Your System\n\n```\noptimal_risk_per_trade = (edge / variance) × (1 / num_concurrent_trades) × safety_factor\n\nWhere:\n- edge = E[R] per trade = 0.375%\n- variance = σ² per trade = 1.548% (in percentage squared)\n- num_concurrent_trades = 2 (average)\n- safety_factor = 0.5 (half-Kelly)\n\n= (0.375 / 1.548) × (1/2) × 0.5\n= 0.242 × 0.5 × 0.5\n= 0.0605 = 6.05% of capital per trade\n```\n\nBut this is Kelly-optimal for a single bet.
